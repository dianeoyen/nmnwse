April 2003 EYH Fund-Raising Report

by Deborah Potter, Vice President / President-Elect NMNWSE

I still have not received all of the biosketches that were requested from each Board member. I´m missing ones from dede, Jeanne, Alexine, Kelly, Dana, Mercedes, Mary and Catherine. Without the information, we will be ineligible for certain grants. Please send them ASAP to ulinski@unm.edu

The Articles of Incorporation were mailed to the Attorney General´s Office, Registrar of Charitable Organizations, on February 20th. That should complete the Network´s paperwork. Registration was required by LANL Foundation and McCune Foundation.

We received a $200 check from Applied Research Associates. Total money raised since October is $5,500 for 2003 and an expected award of $15,000 for subsequent years. Since the last meeting, thank-you letters for donations were mailed to LANL Foundation, United International Engineering, Inc., Applied Research Associates and Office Depot. With the $200 purchase card I bought 800 writeable CD Roms and quarter labels. There was not enough money on the card to buy the jackets, and they were expensive. Perhaps we can buy them later for a better price or get another donation. Who should receive the purchases to copy the career booklets?

Grant applications were mailed to Newman´s Own, Sandia Foundation, NM Women´s Foundation and PNM. Letters of regret were received from New Mexico Children´s Foundation, The Ford Foundation and Burlington Northern. How can we reconstruct a list of past donors? That information is also requested on some grant applications.

Central Chapter has raised questions about the definition of an EYH sponsor. I noted that all cash donors (state-wide and Chapter contributions) should be listed as sponsors, plus organizations that provide materials such as folders. Some people wanted every woman´s work affiliation listed (speakers, tour leaders, planning committee, etc.) including specific Departments and branches of UNM. It seems appropriate that affiliations for women on the EYH Planning Committee are routinely listed (although they are not necessarily sponsors), but I do not recommend that the affiliation of all conference participants be listed. Let´s discuss the definition of EYH sponsor for consistency and Board policy. Do any employers have written agreements with the Network to receive recognition for EYH participation by their employees?
