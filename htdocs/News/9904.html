PRESIDENTS MESSAGE

by Karen Saenz

By now all of our Expanding Your Horizons Conferences are over. I was able to visit with Adrienne at her Conference in Silver City and I did go to Wyona's in El Paso. I also survived the Conference at White Sands on the 18th of March. All were great, positive, learning experiences for the students that attended. I am still receiving positive feedback through the grapevine and am pleased that the Conferences went well, but especially that they are over for this year. I haven't heard about the Albuquerque EYH nor Los Alamos EYH, but assume all went well.

I have been spreading the word, at least in the Southern part of the state, about the Network. I hope to have contacts (and members) from the schools, New Mexico State, and the local businesses. I had lunch with Dr. Linda Riley, NMSU, who has a grant to develop workshops designed to help steer girls to engineering. We discussed combining resources and helping each other reach our very similar goals. I also had several teachers and sponsors at our EYH ask for membership forms and about our meetings. Silver City and WSMR newspapers carried articles about our EYH (did Albuquerque or Los Alamos?) Spreading the word about us is the Network part. As members, we can talk the Network to every source we meet -- you never know where the next member will come from.

Next up is the Science Fair in Socorro, New Mexico. We again will be awarding 5 senior prizes of $50.00 and 5 junior prizes of $30.00 to young women who have excelled in their category.

Plans are in the works to have a 20th Year Anniversary. Flyers should be in the mail soon announcing the event. Please come and bring friends. Also, attached to that flyer is a Bylaw change. We will vote on this change at the Annual Meeting in October.

At the April Board meeting, immediately after the judging, in Socorro, we will be discussing another Procedure change. The issue of the liability insurance that is required (and recommended by the Math/Science Network) needs to be added to Duties and Responsibilities of a Committee Chair. This year, the liability insurance was purchased from an independent insurance agent in Alamogordo and they are more than willing to provide this insurance again next year. Also, the amount on the line item for the State EYH should be increased. If any member has an opinion of how this should be handled, please contact me before the April meeting. Or better yet, bring your opinion to the Board meeting -- all are welcome.

Reminders: 1. If anyone wants information placed on the web site, please send it to Carol La Delfe. 2. The amendment to the Bylaws will be voted on by the membership at the Annual Meeting, 16 October, 1999, and not at the June 6th Board Meeting. If you have sent a vote to Nancy, (Elections Chair), your vote will be kept until October.

See many of you in April.
