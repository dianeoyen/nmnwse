PRESIDENTS MESSAGE

by Kelly Bitner

I became involved in the New Mexico Network for Women in Science and Engineering for the same reason many of you did - to support the Expanding Your Horizons (EYH) conferences. I enthusiastically support these conferences because when I was a young girl I had no way of knowing all the potential careers that could be available for me. The EYH provides a glimpse of the possibilities to the young participants.

However, as a member of the Network, I was somewhat skeptical of the "networking" part of the organization. You know, the "other" part of the Network, that was not the EYH. I hadn't seen much action on that score and had begun to think that it was wishful thinking on the part of the organization's founders. Then I attended the Annual Meeting in Las Cruces in October. I heard an exciting presentation by Liz Frank about a new approach to developing dot matrix plots to match DNA sequences. That was followed by an interesting lunch time talk by Mary O'Connell on the Bio-informatics program at New Mexico State University. Liz and Mary did not know each other prior to the meeting, did not know the other would be there, but they got together after lunch and began the process of getting to know each other and share each other's work. I watched that happen, and realized that networking was happening, right in front of my eyes. This event restored my belief in our organization and renewed my enthusiasm for being a part of it.

With this fresh enthusiasm, I am happy to serve as the President this year. With your help, I hope to renew the enthusiasm of all of our members. Networking can't happen unless we get together and communicate. So let's go to the Chapter meetings, bring new people to Chapter meetings, and above all, talk with everyone, new and familiar, so that everyone walks away feeling welcome and valued!

I would like to recognize the efforts of a number of people in the last year. First, thanks to the Southern Chapter, particularly Nancy Stages and Karen Saenz, for making the Annual Meeting happen. It was a very comfortable yet stimulating event. Second, a big round of applause for Lynda Towers, the outgoing President for her leadership and help during the transition. Third is a big thank you to the board members who continue to take on the extra curricular work of making the organization function. Finally, I would like to recognize the efforts of Judy Mead who regularly empties the Network mail box, sorts the mail and redistributes it to the Board members. Thank you one and all.

Kelly Bitner
