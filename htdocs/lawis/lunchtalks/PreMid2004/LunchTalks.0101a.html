TALK at 12:00:
Laura A. Crotzer, CCS-2, LANL
Time and Learning: An Exploration of a Different School Calendar

ABSTRACT

As a member of the Los Alamos School Board, I have been involved in discussions, explorations, and research into alternative educational approaches that may be able to improve student achievement. One of the concepts currently being examined in some amount of detail is the school calendar. Could a customization of the school calendar, based on the needs and goals of the Los Alamos community, better serve our students??? How might a different calendar look and work??? Originally developed in 1846 when 97% of the nation's population was supported by an agricultural way of life, the school calendar may have become somewhat dated considering the technological world we live in today. Some communities have moved away from the traditional calendar. Thought-provoking ideas, on-going research into student achievement, and how this particular different approach might enable greater student achievement will be presented. It is hoped that this talk will foster on-going, productive discussion within the Lab and local community focused on what practices the community as a whole might use to improve learning opportunities for students of all abilities.

See also the LunchTalk by Joanne Wendelberger on January 18, giving an alternate view on this important topic for parents of school-age children.
