Jewish Women Scientists Around the World

A national exhibit acknowledging
some of the significant contributions
made by these women to the world of science.

March 8th - 25th
Bradbury Science Museum
15th and Central Avenue, Los Alamos, NM

This traveling exhibit profiles sixteen Jewish women scientists and their outstanding efforts in combating disease, understanding the universe, creating new technologies and more. From astronomy to zoology, these scientists have made significant contributions to the world of science. The women featured in this traveling exhibit were from the United States, Europe, Israel, South Africa and Turkey. Two received a Nobel Prize for their work.

Exhibit Opening Events at the Museum - Tuesday March 8

4:30-5:00 p.m. - Isabella Karle, Ph.D., will talk about some of her early work as a chemist for the Manhattan Project. Join the Museum staff and learn about some of her colorful history at that point in time. Dr. Karle is with the Naval Research Laboratory as a Senior Scientist specializing in crystallography. Earlier in the day she is speaking at LANL's Glenn Seaborg Institute for Transactinium Science.

5:00-7:00 p.m. Reception open to the public at the Bradbury Science Museum.

The exhibit was brought to Los Alamos by the Los Alamos Chapter of Hadassah, and is co-sponsored by area Hadassah chapters, Los Alamos Women in Science, the Bradbury Science Museum, and LANL's P Division. The Jewish Women Scientists Around the World Exhibit is a project of the Hadassah-Brandeis Institute, a research center at Brandeis University.

The Museum is open Tuesdays thru Saturdays from 8 a.m. to 5 p.m.
