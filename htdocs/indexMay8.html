The 2005 NMNWSE Annual Meeting was held 28-29 October 2005 in Albuquerque, NM. See the Agendas and Abstracts for details.

Jeanne Banks and Betsy Frederick receive
2005 Governor's Award for Outstanding NM Women

Jeanne's nomination letter emphasizing her contributions to women in our state and her efforts in the American Business Women's Association (ABWA).
Press release about Jeanne's election as national President of ABWA.
Betsy's nomination letter, emphasizing her efforts to improve educational opportunities for girls, and her pivotal role in starting the NMNWSE EYH conferences, which have reached over 15,000 students and their teachers since 1980.
Betsy's biography

Also nominated in 2005 were NMNWSE members Dana Roberson and Tinka Gammel. Previous Governor Award winners include NMNWSE members Kim Linder (2004), Diane Albert (2003, 2000, 1996), Janie Enter (2003), and Debby Potter (2003).

Congratulations to all!

NMNWSE Award Winners in 2004-2005
NM Adventures in SuperComputing Challenge

Local Power Plant Follies by the Shiprock High School Team of Kymberly Brown and Martha Hughes was selected to receive the NMNWSE special award for best project by a woman or team with a majority of women in the 2004-2005 New Mexico Adventures in SuperComputing Challenge. The team was mentored by teachers Vernetta Nobel, Brenda Hines, and Kyla Johnson. NMNWSE is proud to recognize this outstanding project.

NMNWSE also wishes to congratulate all Challenge participants on their many interesting projects!

Nominate members for 2006 Governor's Award

  1. Award the hard work or our members
  2. Encourage involvement in the Network from the nominated members
  3. Support the NM state woman's programs
  4. Help make the Network more visible in the state
  If you would be interested in having NMNWSE nominate you (great!), or would like to recomment another NMNWSE member (please confirm they would be willint to be nominated), please let Tinka Gammel know by Friday, January 6.
  We have had more than one NMNSWE member win this award in a given year, but if we receive several nominee suggestions, we may use some of the nominee suggestions for a different award or in a future year, so NMNWSE nominees aren't "competing against each other".
  Keep in mind, whomever is nominated will have to be willing to write a ~500 word summary on their achievements. The NMNWSE board is willing to assist in this (see jtg@lanl.gov) In order for the Network to submit a packet by the deadline (NMNWSE will pay the application fee and may add additional supporting materials), we need to get achievement summary and supporting materials from the nominee by Thursday, February 16, 2006.
