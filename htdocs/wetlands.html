New MexicoNetwork of Women in Science & Engineering

Please join us in

Summer Bike Tour: constructed wetlands

What: Open Space Alameda Wetland Talk and bike tour along the Rio Grande trail to the Nature Center and back. Elaine Brouillard, Open Space Advisory Board Chair will speak for approximately 15 minutes. Jackie Shane, a board member of GASP (Greater Albuquerque Spokes People) and frequent WOMBAT (Women's Mountain Bike and Tea Society) will assist in our cycling enjoyment.

When: Saturday, June 2 at 1:30 p.m. Yes, we know it will be hot, but the NMNWSE Board of Directors will be able to join us after their meeting and meet old and prospective members. This is a social event to renew and make acquaintances.

Who: Members and prospective members and their families, yes children welcome.

Bring: hats, water, and wearing sunscreen and helmets is encouraged. Bring a picnic lunch to eat at the Nature Center ($1.00 entry fee) if desired. Bike’s must be walked through the Center and parked in a designated zone, so bring your lock.

Where: Alameda Wetlands Open Space facility on Alameda Blvd just west of Rio Grande Blvd. We will meet in the parking lot and walk the short trail to view the wetlands.

Attached map. Questions and RSVPs? Elaine’s e-mail at ebrouill@unm.edu

Jackie Shane will lead a ride to the Wetlands beginning from the UNM area. Call her at 277-5327 or 262-4810 for the meeting place. She will leave from UNM at 12:30 and ride up to the Alameda meeting spot.

Bring whatever type of bicycle you feel comfortable with, at least one water bottle (you can fill up at the Nature Center), and a helmet and sunscreen is recommended. You should also have a pump/patch-kit or slime in your tires so that you are prepared for flats. (Jackie can lend a hand if you flat out).
