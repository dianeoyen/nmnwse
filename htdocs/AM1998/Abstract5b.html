Nondestructive Assay System for the ARIES Weapons Component Dismantlement System
Nancy L. Scheer

Abstract

The Advanced Recovery and Integrated Extraction System (ARIES) was developed at Los Alamos to demonstrate technologies involved in the dismantlement of nuclear weapons components and the conversion of the plutonium to unclassified forms for long term storage or disposition. One module of ARIES is the Nondestructive Assay (NDA) System. This system consists of four computer based nondestructive assay instruments, a robot to load and unload the instruments, and a host computer for central control.

A plutonium sample is moved into the NDA System by a technician; then data such as sample identification and analyses requested will be entered into the host computer. The host computer schedules assays, keeps track of the status of the four instruments, requests loading and unloading of the instruments by the robot, archives results, and prints reports.

The three axes of the robot are arranged as an overhead gantry in a Cartesian configuration. A gripper, force sensor, and digit camera are attached to the z-axis. The force sensor is used to detect collisions and contact with an item to be gripped. The camera is used to read the matrix identification codes ori the top of the containers.

The presentation will discuss the components of the NDA System.
