Mixed Oxide Fuel Fabrication in Support of Surplus Plutonium Disposition
Angelique Neuman

Abstract

There is a present need to reduce the nuclear stockpile and thus reduce the global nuclear danger. The method discussed here is by means of fabricating Mixed Oxide (MOX) Fuel. The PuO2 in this MOX fuel is obtained by converting weapons grade Pu into PuO2 via an oxidation/reduction method. The PuO2 is then thermally treated to remove gallium, an innate impurity of weapons grade plutonium. This thermally treated PuO2 is then mixed with UO2 into a 30-70 wt% primary blend of PuO2-UO2, respectively. Part of this master blend is then reduced to a secondary blend of 5 wt% PuO2. This secondary blend is used to make sintered pellets. The process of pellet fabrication involves, slugging and granulation of the powder, pellet pressing, additive removal, sintering and centerless grinding. The final sintered pellets are inspected and then loaded into pins.
