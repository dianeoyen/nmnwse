Evaluation of Waste Characterization Processes Used to Confirm Acceptable
Knowledge of Transuranic Waste Streams at LANL Facilities

Karen E. Browne

 

Abstract

Los Alamos National Laboratory (LANL) is the first of the Department of Energy (DOE) Research Facilities to receive certification authority to ship Transuranic (TRW) Waste to the Waste Isolation Pilot Plant (WIPP). WIPP is a long-term geologic disposal facility for TRU waste, located in Carlsbad, New Mexico. TRU waste contains alpha-emitting particles with an atomic number of greater than 92, has a half-life of more than 20 years, and are present in concentrations of greater than 100 nanocuries (nCi/g) per gram of waste. All waste that is designated to be stored at the WIPP facility must be defense related. It can contain heavy metals and organic compounds regulated in the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act (RCRA) once a RCRA Part B permit is issued by the State of New Mexico Environment Department. It can not be low-level radioactive waste. Low-level radioactive waste is defined as waste that contains no more than 100 nCi/g of alpha-emitting particles (i.e., Plutonium, Americium, Uranium, etc.).

The Transuranic Waste Characterization/Certification Program (TWCP) here at LANL is regulated by the DOE, U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), New Mexico Environment Department (NMED), and the Carlsbad Area Office (CAO). Periodic audits are conducted by these regulatory agencies to ensure that the TWCP is following the procedures they have published and are in compliance with the requirements set by the regulating parties. The scope of these audits includes waste characterization process, data analysis, acceptable knowledge documentation and confirmation, packaging and transportation, and waste documentation.
