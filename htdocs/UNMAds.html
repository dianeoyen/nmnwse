CIVIL ENGINEERING DEPARTMENT INFORMATION

AGC Chaired Professor and Assistant Professor

The Civil Engineering Department at the University of New Mexico invites applications for two tenure-track faculty positions. The first position is the New Mexico Building Branch, Associated General Contractors (AGC) Endowed Chair in Construction, to be filled at the Associate/Full Professor level. The second position is in structural engineering and construction, at the Assistant Professor level. The positions start August 2001.

  • JR 5094A

    • The AGC Endowed Chair: position is at the rank of Associate or Full Professor. This senior leadership position in the construction engineering and construction management program will be filled by a distinguished individual with demonstrated relevant experience.

     

    Minimum Qualifications: An earned Ph.D. degree by appointment start date in Construction Engineering, Construction Management or in Civil Engineering with a Construction emphasis as well as industry and academic experience is required. The individual must be a licensed professional engineer or qualified for licensure.

     

    Preferred Qualifications: A strong record of scholarly productivity and a demonstrated ability to work cooperatively with a diverse group of stakeholders at the University and in the construction industry are desired.

     

  • JR 5094B

    • Assistant Professor: The second position is in the area of construction and structural engineering.

     

    Minimum Qualifications: Successful candidates must have a Ph.D. degree by appointment start date in Civil Engineering or Construction Engineering.

     

    Preferred Qualifications: Desired qualifications include the potential for research, teaching and service leading to tenure at the University, the ability to teach in and contribute to the construction management/construction engineering and structural engineering programs.
     
    The position is at the Assistant Professor level, but applicants who qualify for Associate Professor will be considered.

 

The University of New Mexico is a Carnegie Doctoral/Research Extensive University and a Hispanic Serving Institution with approximately 30,000 students on the main and branch campuses. One of five departments in the School of Engineering, Civil Engineering currently has 14 tenure-track faculty members. The department is committed to excellence in both undergraduate and graduate education, with the B.S. programs in civil and construction engineering accredited by the Engineering Accrediting Committee of the Accreditation Board for Engineering and Technology, and the B.S. degree program in construction management accredited by the American Council for Construction Education. The department offers M.S. and Ph.D. programs involving students in leading-edge research. More information can be found through the School of Engineering web site at http://www.cs.unm.edu/soe/.

 

How to Apply: Please submit a letter of interest, a detailed curriculum vitae and the names, mailing addresses, and e-mail addresses of at least five professional references for the first, and three for the second, position. Send all submissions to: Professor Gerald W. May, Civil Engineering Department, University of New Mexico, Albuquerque, NM, 87131. Email: gmay@unm.edu. Electronic and fax submissions will be accepted when followed by a hard copy within 24 hours. For best consideration, applications must be received by January 15, 2001. The candidate pools will remain open until the positions are filled. Representatives of all underrepresented groups are encouraged to apply.

COMPUTER SCIENCE DEPARTMENT INFORMATION

JR 5108 AB
The Department of Computer Science at the University of New Mexico is conducting a search to fill two or more tenure-track faculty positions at all ranks. We are interested in hiring outstanding faculty members whose research interests complement and augment existing strengths in the department. Areas of particular interest include networks, mobile and distributed computing, computational biology, software system design and analysis, and graphics and visualization, but all areas will be considered. Preference will be given to candidates who have a strong interest in interdisciplinary research.

Minimum Qualifications: Candidates must have completed, or be about to complete, a doctorate in CS or a relevant area by September 1, 2001.

 

Applicants should demonstrate a strong commitment to undergraduate and graduate education and the ability to establish a nationally visible research program.

 

The Department of Computer Science currently has 20 tenure-track positions. Active research programs include: adaptive computation, computational biology, computer immunology, physics and computation, computational neuroscience, tomography and brain imaging, artificial intelligence, automated reasoning, formal methods, symbolic and algebraic computation, data mining, experimental algorithmics, scalable systems and high-performance networks, high-performance compilers and run-time systems, scientific computing and visualization. The department has internationally recognized research programs in several of these areas and enjoys strong research collaborations with the Santa Fe Institute and Sandia and Los Alamos National Laboratories. The department has strong inter-disciplinary research activities jointly with the departments of Biology, Physics, Neuroscience, Medical School, Psychology and Linguistics. The department also has a strong association with the Albuquerque High Performance Computing Center, a member of the National Computational Science Alliance (NCSA).

 

The Department of Computer Science is one of five departments in the School of Engineering at the University of New Mexico. With approximately 200 undergraduate students, the department offers a BS in CS, which is accredited by CSAB. The department also offers the M.S. and Ph.D. with approximately 100 graduate students currently enrolled. The University of New Mexico has a student enrollment of approximately 25,000 and is a Hispanic Serving, Doctoral/Research University-Extensive. For further information about the Computer Science Department at the University of New Mexico, see: http://www.cs.unm.edu

 

How to Apply: For best consideration, applications must be received by January 15, 2001, although we will continue to accept applications until the positions are filled. Each application should include a curriculum vita, a research statement, a teaching statement, and the names of four references. A cover letter should summarize your experience and indicate the level of the appointment you are seeking. Printed applications should be sent to:

 

Arthur B. (Barney) Maccabe

Chair, Faculty Search Committee

Department of Computer Science

The University of New Mexico

Albuquerque, NM 87131-1386
